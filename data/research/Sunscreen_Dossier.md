## Executive Summary: New Product Research Study on Sunscreen

### Research Topic: Sunscreen Innovation and Market Analysis

### Key Findings:

1. **Core Functions of Sunscreen:**
   - Sunscreen effectively prevents skin cancer and melanoma.
   - Different types of sunscreen include broad-spectrum sunscreens that provide UVA protection proportional to UVB protection.
   - Sunscreen works by preventing UV damage through the use of a nanoparticle delivery vehicle.


2. **Ideal Product/Service/Experience:**
   - The ideal sunscreen should meet international regulation criteria, address unmet consumer needs, and incorporate the latest sunscreen technology trends.
   - Unmet consumer needs include the lack of international regulation, nanoparticles not being defined as appropriate for sunscreen use by the USFDA, and the absence of natural organic sunscreens in approved sunscreen filters.
   - Latest trends in sunscreen technology include inorganic ingredients, cyclodextrins, and natural organic sunscreens.


3. **Consumer Satisfaction:**
   - Factors influencing consumer satisfaction with sunscreen include broad-spectrum sunscreen criteria, underapplication of sunscreen, and tints and ingredients for particular skin care needs.
   - Common complaints about sunscreen include the lack of international regulation, stinging eyes, and unpleasant feel or smell.
   - Improving consumer satisfaction involves using clear language on the label and ensuring the product is easy to apply.


4. **Current Gaps and Challenges:**
   - Current gaps in the sunscreen market include the lack of international regulation, undefined status of nanoparticles, and the need for more data on currently approved “organic filters”.
   - Challenges facing the sunscreen industry include the lack of international regulation criterion, the need for more data on currently approved “organic filters”, and the undefined status of nanoparticles.


5. **Opportunities for Innovation:**
   - Opportunities for innovation in the sunscreen market include tinted sunscreens with skincare benefits, new sunscreen actives, international regulation of sunscreen nanoparticles, and nanoparticles in sunscreens.


6. **Market Trends:**
   - Emerging trends in the sunscreen market include the effectiveness of sunscreen in preventing skin cancer and melanoma, the lack of one international regulation criterion for sunscreens, and the USFDA not defining nanoparticles as appropriate for sunscreen use.
   - Long-term trends in the sunscreen market include the proven effectiveness of sunscreen in preventing skin cancer and melanoma.


7. **Key Technology and Scientific Advancements:**
   - Key technologies and scientific advancements in the sunscreen industry include tinted sunscreens, diethylaminohydroxylbenzoylhexyl benzoate and ethylhexyl triazone, and avobenzone.
   - These technologies and advancements are changing the sunscreen market by introducing new sunscreen actives, improving the effectiveness of sunscreens, and addressing unmet consumer needs.
   - The potential implications of these technologies and advancements for the future of the sunscreen market include the development of more effective and innovative sunscreens that meet consumer needs and regulatory requirements.
